Hello again, SharePoint Site Administrators...

You are being contacted because you are listed as an administrator for an existing site collection hosted in our centrally-managed SharePoint service at https://sharepoint.uvm.edu.  We are planning to upgrade this instance of SharePoint from the current "2010 Foundation" version to the latest "2013 Standard Edition" sometime in the late Spring to early Summer of 2015.

Given the significant visual and functional changes in SharePoint 2013, we want to give you a chance to preview the new look and feel of your site before the final upgrade.  You now can view an upgraded, read-only copy of your site at the following URL:
-siteURL-

If you find that part (or all) of your upgraded site is not working properly, please send a help request to " saa-ad@uvm.edu", and we will open a support ticket to track your problem.

Some people may find it necessary to have write access to their sites in order to complete testing of the site upgrades.  If this is the case for you, send a site unlock request to the email address above.  Please keep in mind that any data written to these test sites will not be preserved.  Any customizations or new data written to the test sites will be destroyed during the final upgrade process.

The upgrade will involve several major changes to SharePoint:

  • Upgrade of the core technology from "SharePoint 2010 Foundation" to "SharePoint 2013 Standard"
    (see: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/support/whats-new-in-microsoft-sharepoint-server-2013-HA102785546.aspx.
    and: http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/jj819267.aspx#bkmk_FeaturesOnPremise).
  • A full visual upgrade of all existing sites to SharePoint 2013 themes.
  • Replacement of Windows-integrated authentication with UVM's unified "WebLogin" interface.
  • Introduction of "Managed Metadata Services" as part of the Standard Edition upgrade.
    (See: http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ee424402(v=office.15).aspx)

At present, GuestNet authentication is not working, but we will have this feature in place and available for testing before the final upgrade.

We feel it is important that this upgrade be performed by early Summer of 2014 to ensure optimum compatibility between SharePoint and the new mail and calendar service.  Since there is relatively little time left in the semester, please try to report any problems with your upgraded site as soon as possible.  Early reporting will help us to address any critical site issues before the final upgrade.
